Title:

: Delegation games: Choosing strategy over Tactics

Abstract:

Market games between firms - producing similar goods - are (typically) played with production levels or market prices as firms' strategies. In this talk, I will first introduce basics of game theory: games, equilibrium and related computational problems. Subsequently, I will talk about equilibria in market delegation games and show how firms benefit by delegating their games.


This talk will not assume any background in Mathematical Economics and will be self contained.
